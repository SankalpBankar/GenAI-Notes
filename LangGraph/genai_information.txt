Generative AI (GenAI) - Overview

1.  What is Generative AI? Generative AI refers to artificial
    intelligence systems that can create new content such as text,
    images, audio, video, and code. It learns patterns from large
    datasets and generates outputs that resemble human-created data.

2.  How it works Most GenAI models are based on deep learning
    architectures like Transformers. They are trained on massive
    datasets and use probability to predict the next token in a
    sequence.

3.  Popular Models

-   GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer)
-   DALL·E (Image generation models)
-   Stable Diffusion
-   Midjourney
-   LLaMA

4.  Applications

-   Chatbots and virtual assistants
-   Content creation (blogs, ads, scripts)
-   Code generation and debugging
-   Image, audio, and video synthesis
-   Healthcare and education support

5.  Advantages

-   Automates creative work
-   Saves time
-   Enhances productivity
-   Assists in learning and research

6.  Limitations

-   May produce incorrect or biased outputs
-   Requires large compute resources
-   Ethical concerns (misuse, deepfakes)

7.  Future Scope Generative AI will become more multimodal, accurate,
    and integrated into everyday tools across industries.
